Overview

Alzheimer's disease is a progressive neurological disorder that causes brain cells to degenerate and die. It is the most common cause of dementia, affecting millions worldwide.

Symptoms

  • Memory loss
  • Difficulty in planning and problem-solving
  • Confusion with time or place
  • Mood and personality changes

Causes and Risk Factors

Alzheimer's disease is influenced by genetic, lifestyle, and environmental factors. Age and family history are the primary risk factors.

Treatment

Although there is no cure, certain treatments can help slow the progression of symptoms and improve the quality of life for patients.

Prevention

A healthy lifestyle, regular mental activity, and exercise may reduce the risk of Alzheimer's.
